# PRODUCT — Aquad'or

The "who and why" every road agent reads before designing or building. Required substrate per the framework — planner, builder, verifier all load this.

---

## Register

**Mixed: primary `product`, with `brand` chrome on top.**

- **Product register dominates** — this is an e-commerce app. The cart works, the checkout settles, the admin updates inventory. Design serves the product flow on shop / PDP / cart / checkout / admin.
- **Brand register surfaces** on the homepage, journal, brand story sections, custom-perfume builder pitch, AI concierge. These are *moments of seduction* — design IS the product there.

The seduction is what gets the buyer to the cart. The product is what closes them. Both registers ship in v3.0.

## Three real users

(Specific humans + workflow, not personas.)

### 1. Maria, 38 · Limassol · gift buyer
Mid-shopping-list mode. Found Aquad'or via Instagram, opening on iPhone in a beachfront café before her anniversary dinner. Wants to buy a 100ml signature in under 6 minutes. She doesn't read product copy — she scans for "this is the one" cues: photo, price, "ships in 2-3 days". If the PDP makes her think, she bounces. If the page makes her *feel* without slowing her down, she completes.

**Implication:** PDP needs a glance-readable hero (image + price + Add-to-Cart in the first 700px of mobile). Editorial copy lives below, never blocking the conversion path. Apple Pay must work on first tap.

### 2. Khaled, 29 · Dubai expat in Nicosia · niche connoisseur
Browse mode. iPad in the evening, oud-deep, comparing Lattafa to a Maison Francis Kurkdjian he sniffed last week. Reads every notes pyramid. Cross-references brands. Wants the AI concierge because he likes being asked the right question.

**Implication:** PDP needs depth: full notes pyramid (top/heart/base), brand story, similar-scent recommendations, accord descriptors. Niche category needs first-class filters (brand, family, accord). Concierge needs to feel literate, not chatbot-flat.

### 3. Eleni, 45 · Nicosia · boutique reorder
Repeat customer. Owns a small wellness boutique and resells signature pieces to clients. Logs in once a month, reorders three or four items she's bought before. Wants to be done in 90 seconds. Hates re-typing addresses.

**Implication:** Customer account area with reorder history + one-click reorder + saved address + saved payment. Admin needs the "regular customer LTV" visible so we can identify wholesale-prone buyers.

## Brand voice

**Three adjectives:** editorial · restrained · sensual.

**One paragraph in motion** (an empty-cart message, an error, a confirmation):

> *Empty cart:* "Nothing in here yet. Three things people are wearing this week →"
>
> *Add-to-cart error:* "We couldn't add this just now. Try once more — and if it persists, write us."
>
> *Order confirmation:* "It's ours to send now. You'll have it in three days, maybe four. The notes settle best after the first wear; let it find its skin."

Note the absence of: exclamation marks, emoji, "Awesome!" / "Oops!" tone, capital-letter shouting, blue/red banner alarms. We don't *announce*; we *address*.

## Three anti-references (do NOT look like)

### 1. Sephora.com / Ulta.com
Every product, every banner, every "Save 20%!" — the visual screams. Multi-pixel-density gold sale stickers. The page is a sales floor. Aquad'or is the opposite: the page is a curated table.

**Why anti:** the moment Aquad'or looks like a mainstream beauty retailer, the price premium evaporates. Our buyers are paying for the editorial framing, not the discount.

### 2. Generic Shopify perfume stores
You know the look: Glossier-coded soft pastels, rounded cards, "Hey beautiful!" copy, three identical product cards with "Best Seller" tags. The Shopify default theme + a perfume photo.

**Why anti:** indistinguishable. The page could be selling candles, skincare, or pet food. Aquad'or has a specific cultural placement (Levant-coded, niche-leaning) — the design must declare it.

### 3. Casino-luxury gold-on-black perfume sites
Cliché territory. Gold serif on jet black, glitter particle animation in the hero, "EXCLUSIVE LUXURY" in serif caps. Reads as Vegas, not Levant.

**Why anti:** the previous Aquad'or (v1.2/v2.0) drifted toward this. v3.0 is explicitly the opposite: warm bone surfaces, restrained gold accent, no glitter, no neon. Editorial paper, not casino chip.

## One-sentence differentiation

**"Reading a perfume's page felt like opening a letter from someone who knows scent."**

That's what someone says 24 hours after their first visit. If they say "nice site, easy to use" — we shipped a feature, not a brand. If they say "fast checkout" — we shipped commerce, not seduction. The line we want is the one above.

## Decision rules for downstream agents

When in doubt during build:

- **Copy:** restrained, editorial, lowercase trending. Never exclamation. Never emoji. Address the reader, don't announce at them.
- **Color:** when picking between two options, the more muted wins. When picking between a tinted neutral and a bright color, neutral wins. Gold is a accent, not a fill.
- **Layout:** when picking between dense (more on screen) and generous (more whitespace), generous wins. We're editorial.
- **Animation:** when picking between fast/snappy and slow/considered, slow wins. We're not a fintech.
- **Conversion vs aesthetic:** they're the same thing. A page that converts is a page that doesn't make the buyer think. Editorial restraint *is* conversion restraint. The "two clear paths through this page" rule applies.
